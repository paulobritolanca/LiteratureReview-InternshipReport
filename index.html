The Relevance of Accounting in the Music Sector

An advanced investigation into accounting challenges in revenue recognition, intangible assets and royalty distribution in the era of AI-powered music streaming platforms.

Author

Paulo Monteiro

Institution

University of Minho

Date

June 2025

Research Summary and Internship Overview

Objective: To investigate the relevance of accounting in the digital music sector, focusing on challenges related to revenue recognition, intangible assets and royalty distribution. Also describe the practical activities carried out in the curricular internship at Bright Assets.

Methodology: Bibliographic review of articles, standards (such as IFRS 15), tax legislation and emerging technologies (e.g. blockchain); and practical description of internship activities.

Results: The digitization of the music industry has introduced new accounting complexities, especially in revenue recognition and rights management. The user-centric royalty distribution model proved more equitable for less popular artists compared to the pro-rata model. The application of IFRS 15 contributed to greater comparability of financial statements. Although blockchain has potential to improve copyright management, it still faces standardization challenges. The activities developed in the internship allowed consolidating practical skills in accounting, taxation and internal control.

Conclusions: Accounting is crucial for the sustainability of the digital music sector, promoting transparency and technological adaptation. The practical experience reinforced the importance of technical rigor, professional ethics and innovation to face the challenges of a constantly evolving industry.

Keywords: Accounting · Music sector · Streaming · IFRS 15 · Blockchain · Royalties · Taxation · Curricular internship
